# Trading Fundamentals

## Market Participants

### Investors
- Long-term holders
- Focus on fundamentals
- Buy and hold strategy

### Traders
- Short to medium term
- Focus on price movements
- Active buying/selling

### Market Makers
- Provide liquidity
- Quote bid and ask prices
- Profit from spread

---

## Order Types

### Market Order
- Buy/sell immediately at current price
- Guaranteed execution, not price
- Use when: Speed matters more than price

### Limit Order
- Buy/sell at specific price or better
- Guaranteed price, not execution
- Use when: Price matters more than speed

### Stop Order (Stop Loss)
- Becomes market order when price hits trigger
- Protect against losses
- Example: "Sell if price drops below $50"

### Stop-Limit Order
- Becomes limit order when trigger hit
- More control, less certainty of execution

---

## Market Mechanics

### Bid & Ask
- **Bid**: Highest price buyer will pay
- **Ask**: Lowest price seller will accept
- **Spread**: Difference between bid and ask

### Order Book
Queue of all buy and sell orders at different prices.

```
     ASKS (Sellers)
$102.50  |  500 shares
$102.00  |  1000 shares
$101.50  |  750 shares
---------+-----------
$101.00  |  800 shares
$100.50  |  1200 shares
$100.00  |  600 shares
     BIDS (Buyers)
```

## Settlement

### T+1 Settlement
- Trade today, settle tomorrow
- Modern standard (India, US moving here)

### T+2 Settlement
- Trade today, settle in 2 business days
- Traditional standard

### What "Settle" Means
- Transfer of shares to buyer
- Transfer of money to seller
